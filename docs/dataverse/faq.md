# Dataverse FAQ

This page answers common questions about UCLA Dataverse, including deposits, access, accounts, and general repository features. The questions are grouped by category for quick reference.

---

# Understanding Dataverse

## What is Dataverse?
{: .toggle}
Dataverse is open source software for publishing, citing, and preserving research data. It was created by the Institute for Quantitative Social Science at Harvard University. Dataverse installations can exchange metadata and connect with external systems, which supports broader data reuse.

## What is UCLA Dataverse?
{: .toggle}
UCLA Dataverse is the research data repository for UCLA. It supports faculty, staff, and students who need to archive or publish data. The platform is operated by the UCLA Library Data Science Center and runs on the Dataverse software.

## What is the difference between a dataverse and a dataset?
{: .toggle}
A **dataverse** is a container. It can hold datasets or other dataverses.  
A **dataset** is a published package that can include data files, code, documentation, and metadata.

Most researchers create a dataverse to organize multiple datasets in one location.

# Getting Started

## How do I log in?
{: .toggle}
Go to <https://dataverse.ucla.edu> and choose **University of California, Los Angeles**.  
Use your UCLA Logon ID to sign in.

## Who can create a dataverse?
{: .toggle}
Any UCLA faculty, staff, or student with a UCLA Logon ID can create a dataverse.

## How do I create a dataverse?
{: .toggle}
After logging in:

1. Select **Add Data**  
2. Choose **New Dataverse**  
3. Add a title, a short description, and a subject area  
4. Save the new dataverse

This becomes your main space for managing datasets.

## How do I publish my first dataset?
{: .toggle}
Inside your dataverse:

1. Select **Add Data**  
2. Choose **New Dataset**  
3. Upload your data files, documentation, or code  
4. Complete the required metadata fields  
5. Save and publish when ready

You can also keep the dataset restricted until you approve access requests.

# Depositing Data

## What file types can I upload?
{: .toggle}
Most common research formats are supported, including tabular data, images, text, code, and geospatial files. Very large or complex formats may need help from the Data Science Center.

## Can I update a dataset after publishing?
{: .toggle}
Yes. Dataverse supports versioning.  
You can add or replace files, update metadata, and publish a new version.  
Earlier versions remain available.

---

# Access and Permissions

## Can I restrict access to my data?
{: .toggle}
Yes. You can:

- Keep files restricted  
- Approve or deny access requests  
- Share files privately  
- Add custom terms for data access

## Can I share data only with collaborators?
{: .toggle}
Yes. You can assign permissions to specific users or groups.

# Support and Help

## Who do I contact for help?
{: .toggle}
Email the UCLA Data Science Center at **dsc@ucla.edu** for help with deposits, metadata, formats, permissions, or policy questions.

## Is training available?
{: .toggle}
Yes. The Data Science Center offers workshops, consultations, and course-integrated instruction. We can help prepare data for publication.
